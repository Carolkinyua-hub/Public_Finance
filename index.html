Our Motivation

Bondarenko(n.d) describes a debt crisis as a situation where a country is unable to pay back its Government Debt.

This comes about when tax revenues of its Government are less that expenditures for a prolonged period.

The expectation is that Government ought to sufficiently finance expenditures by raising money through taxation.

However, when tax revenues are not sufficient , the government can make up the difference by issuing debt by selling treasury bills in an open market to investors.

Bondarenko goes ahead to assert that if the Government is able to make good of its payments its not difficult for it to find investors willing to lend it money.

However if the burden of debt becomes huge , investors worry about the ability to pay back and thereby demand high interest rates to cushion themselves against the high risk lending.

This increased worry by investors may intensify thereby leading to a vicious cycle of increased cost of lending and could penultimately increase the probability of the country defaulting on existing debt and ultimately entering a debt crisis.
